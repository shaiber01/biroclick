# Assumptions Log: Eizner et al. 2015 Reproduction

This file tracks all assumptions made during the reproduction process.

---

## Format

Each assumption is documented as:
- **ID:** Unique identifier
- **Parameter:** What is being assumed
- **Value:** The assumed value
- **Rationale:** Why this assumption is reasonable
- **Impact:** Critical / Moderate / Minor
- **Affects:** Which stages/figures
- **Source:** "Paper" or "Agent assumption"

---

## Assumptions

### A1: Resolution
- **Parameter:** FDTD grid resolution
- **Value:** 5 nm
- **Rationale:** Smallest feature is 25 nm (nanorod minor axis); need at least 5 points across. At λ=400 nm in glass (n=1.51), λ/n ≈ 265 nm, so 5 nm gives ~53 points per wavelength (well above λ/20 rule).
- **Impact:** Moderate (affects accuracy and runtime)
- **Affects:** All stages
- **Source:** Agent assumption

---

### A2: PML Thickness
- **Parameter:** Perfectly matched layer thickness
- **Value:** 1.0 µm (200 grid points at 5 nm resolution)
- **Rationale:** Standard for visible FDTD; sufficient to absorb reflections
- **Impact:** Minor
- **Affects:** All stages
- **Source:** Agent assumption

---

### A3: Simulation Domain Height (z)
- **Parameter:** Total z-extent of simulation domain
- **Value:** ~3 µm (1 µm PML bottom, 0.5 µm glass, structure, 0.5 µm superstrate, 1 µm PML top)
- **Rationale:** Need space for source, monitors, and PML on both sides
- **Impact:** Minor
- **Affects:** All stages
- **Source:** Agent assumption

---

### A4: Period Interpretation
- **Parameter:** Meaning of "Γ = 180 nm side-to-side separation"
- **Value:** Center-to-center period = D + 180 nm
- **Rationale:** Paper explicitly states "side to side separation, Γ" meaning the gap between disks is 180 nm. Therefore, for D=140 nm disk, period = 140 + 180 = 320 nm.
- **Impact:** Critical (affects plasmon resonance position)
- **Affects:** Stages 2-5
- **Source:** Paper (interpreted)

---

### A5: Al Optical Data
- **Parameter:** Aluminum permittivity model
- **Value:** Drude-Lorentz fit to Palik data
- **Rationale:** Paper cites Palik (ref 60). Will use established Drude-Lorentz parameters for Al.
- **Impact:** Critical
- **Affects:** All stages
- **Source:** Paper + agent implementation

---

### A6: ITO Optical Data
- **Parameter:** ITO permittivity model
- **Value:** Drude model with ε∞=3.9, ωp=1.78×10^15 rad/s, γ=1.5×10^14 rad/s
- **Rationale:** Paper cites Sopra database; using standard ITO Drude parameters from literature
- **Impact:** Moderate
- **Affects:** All stages
- **Source:** Agent assumption (literature values)

---

### A7: TDBC Lorentz Model
- **Parameter:** TDBC permittivity
- **Value:** ε(ω) = ε∞ + f·ωX²/(ωX² - ω² - iγXω) with ε∞=2.56, ωX=3.22×10^15 rad/s, γX=2.45×10^13 rad/s, f=0.45
- **Rationale:** Explicitly given in paper Methods section
- **Impact:** Critical
- **Affects:** Stages 4-6
- **Source:** Paper (direct)

---

### A8: Source Type and Spectrum
- **Parameter:** Excitation source
- **Value:** Broadband plane wave, 400-800 nm, normal incidence, x-polarized
- **Rationale:** Transmission spectra over visible range; normal incidence implied by no angle mention; polarization along x for disk symmetry
- **Impact:** Moderate
- **Affects:** All stages
- **Source:** Paper (implied) + agent assumption

---

### A9: Nanodisk Shape
- **Parameter:** Geometry of Al nanodisk
- **Value:** Cylinder with rounded edges (radius ~5 nm)
- **Rationale:** E-beam lithography typically produces rounded features; will start with sharp cylinder and note if edge rounding is needed
- **Impact:** Minor
- **Affects:** Stages 2-6
- **Source:** Agent assumption

---

### A10: Simulation Time
- **Parameter:** FDTD run duration
- **Value:** Until fields decay to 10^-4 of peak, or maximum 500 fs
- **Rationale:** Need sufficient time for resonances to develop and fields to decay
- **Impact:** Moderate
- **Affects:** All stages
- **Source:** Agent assumption

---

### A11: Native Oxide Layer
- **Parameter:** Al2O3 layer on Al surface
- **Value:** Neglected (not modeled)
- **Rationale:** Paper mentions 2-3 nm native oxide but FDTD Methods section doesn't include it explicitly. Including it would add complexity without clear parameters.
- **Impact:** Minor (may cause small resonance shifts)
- **Affects:** All stages
- **Source:** Agent assumption

---

### A12: Diameter Values for Sweep
- **Parameter:** Nanodisk diameters to simulate
- **Value:** 80, 100, 120, 140, 160, 180, 200 nm (7 values)
- **Rationale:** Paper shows ~7 diameters in Fig. 3c,d; covers range from before to after exciton crossing
- **Impact:** Minor
- **Affects:** Stages 3, 5
- **Source:** Agent assumption (coarser than paper for efficiency)
